Table Tennis

Men Team

  • Format Of Play: Singles(A vs X), Singles (B vs Y), Doubles, Reverse Singles(A vs Y), Reverse Singles(B vs X).
  • All games will be best of 5.
  • Players playing singles cannot play the doubles.
  • Maximum 5 and minimum 4 players are allowed in a team.

Women Team

  • Format Of Play: Singles(A vs X), Singles (B vs Y), Singles(C vs Z), Reverse Singles(A vs Y), Reverse Singles(B vs X).
  • All games will be best of 5.
  • 3 separate players are required in the team.
  • Maximum 4 and minimum 3 players are allowed in a team.

All standard rules as followed by the ITTF are applicable.

Any show of unnecessary aggression or unsportsmanlike conduct towards the umpire or the playing equipment will lead to the immediate disqualification of the player. Any discussions regarding the same will not be entertained.

Lawn Tennis

  • Each team should have minimum 3 and maximum 4 players.
  • Tournament type will be decided later on the basis of number of teams registered.
  • Qualifying stages will consist of one singles match and one doubles match.
  • A singles match will be played after the first two matches ONLY in the event of a tie.
  • Seeding will be done based on the team’s performance in the qualifiers stage and will be done by the organizing committee.
  • Teams should report to the tennis court half an hour before their match. Any disciplinary violation by any member of the team will lead to disqualification of that team. Teams should bring their own kit. Only match balls will be provided for the matches. The chair umpire has final words on all issues related to on court facts. All the team members are required to show their ID cards before the beginning of the match failing which the defaulting individual will not be allowed to play. The Rules of Tennis 2013 as released by the ITF will be followed.
  • In case of a deuce, the first side which scores two points wins the game. These points need not be consecutive.
  • The qualifying stage matches(singles) will be best of 7 games each. If and when both sides win a minimum of three games each, a tiebreaker round will be played to decide the outcome of the match.
  • The qualifying stage matches(doubles) will be best of 13 games each. If and when both sides win a minimum of six games each, a tiebreaker round will be played to decide the outcome of the match.
  • The semifinals and finals will be best of 3 sets each. Each set will consist of 7 games. If and when both sides win a minimum of three games each, a tiebreaker round will be played to decide the outcome of the match
  • Above rules can be modified or deleted by the organizing commitee at any point of time without prior notice.
